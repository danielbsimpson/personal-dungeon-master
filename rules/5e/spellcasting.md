# D&D 5e SRD — Spellcasting Rules

*Source: System Reference Document 5.1 (CC-BY-4.0, Wizards of the Coast)*

---

## What Is a Spell?

A spell is a discrete magical effect — a single shaping of magical energy into a specific form. Spells have a level (0–9), a school, a casting time, a range, components, a duration, and a description of their effect.

---

## Spell Levels and Slots

Spells are categorized by **level** (0 through 9). Higher-level spells are more powerful.

**Level 0 spells are called cantrips.** They can be cast any number of times without expending a spell slot.

Spells of level 1 and above require a **spell slot** to cast. A spell slot is expended when a spell is cast. Spell slots are recovered on a **long rest** (for most classes).

### Upcasting

A spell can be cast at a higher slot level than its base level. The spell's effect is enhanced as described in its text ("*At Higher Levels.*"). A slot of the required level or higher must be available.

### Spell Slots by Class (Selected)

The DM should reference the full class tables for exact slot counts at each level. Representative examples at character level 5:

| Class | Slot Levels Available |
|---|---|
| Wizard, Sorcerer | 1st (4), 2nd (3), 3rd (2) |
| Cleric, Druid | 1st (4), 2nd (3), 3rd (2) |
| Bard | 1st (4), 2nd (3), 3rd (2) |
| Paladin, Ranger | 1st (4), 2nd (2) |
| Fighter (Eldritch Knight), Rogue (Arcane Trickster) | 1st (3) |
| Warlock | 3rd (2) — always recovered on short rest |

## Cantrips

- Cantrips are free to cast — no spell slot required.
- A cantrip's effect cannot be changed by spending a spell slot.
- Many cantrips scale with character level (not class level), typically gaining additional dice at levels 5, 11, and 17.

---

## Spellcasting Ability Modifier and Save DC

Each spellcasting class uses a specific ability score:

| Class | Spellcasting Ability |
|---|---|
| Wizard | Intelligence |
| Cleric, Druid, Ranger | Wisdom |
| Bard, Paladin, Sorcerer, Warlock | Charisma |

**Spell Save DC** = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier

**Spell Attack Bonus** = proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier

---

## Spell Attack Rolls

Some spells require the caster to make an **attack roll** to see if the spell hits.

- Roll 1d20 + spell attack bonus vs. the target's AC.
- A natural 20 is a critical hit — roll the damage dice twice.
- Spell attack rolls follow all the normal rules for attack rolls (advantage, disadvantage, cover, etc.).
- Spells that require a spell attack roll **do not allow a saving throw** on a hit.

---

## Concentration

Some spells require the caster to maintain **concentration** to keep the effect active.

**Concentration rules:**
- A caster can maintain concentration on only **one spell at a time**. Casting a second concentration spell immediately ends the first.
- Concentration can be ended voluntarily at any time (no action required).
- Taking damage while concentrating requires a **Constitution saving throw**: DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher. On failure, the spell ends.
- Being **incapacitated** or **killed** immediately ends concentration.
- Environmental hazards (being buffeted by waves, being shaken by an earthquake) may require Constitution saving throws as well.

---

## Ritual Casting

A spell with the *ritual* tag can be cast as a **ritual**.

- Ritual casting takes **10 additional minutes** beyond the spell's normal casting time.
- A ritual **does not expend a spell slot**.
- Wizards can ritual-cast any ritual spell in their spellbook (even if not currently prepared).
- Clerics, Druids, and Bards can only ritual-cast rituals they have prepared/known.

---

## Components

Spells require one or more of the following components:

| Component | Abbreviation | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Verbal (V)** | V | Must be able to speak; silence or *Silence* spell prevents this |
| **Somatic (S)** | S | Must have a free hand to gesture |
| **Material (M)** | M | Must have the specific material or a spellcasting focus |

A **spellcasting focus** (arcane focus, holy symbol, druidic focus) can substitute for any material component that has no listed cost and is not consumed by the spell.

If a material component has a listed gold cost, it must be provided in the listed form. A focus cannot substitute.

## Casting Time

| Casting Time | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 action | Cast on your turn as your action |
| 1 bonus action | Cast on your turn as your bonus action; any other spell cast the same turn must be a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action |
| 1 reaction | Cast immediately in response to the specified trigger |
| 1 minute or longer | Must use your action each round for the required time; concentration is required throughout; interruption (damage or distraction) requires CON save |

**Bonus action spellcasting rule:** If you cast a spell as a bonus action, the only other spell you can cast on that turn is a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action.

## Counterspell and Dispel Magic

### Counterspell (3rd Level)
- Cast as a **reaction** when a creature within 60 ft casts a spell.
- Automatically counters spells of 3rd level and lower.
- For spells of 4th level or higher: make an **ability check using your spellcasting ability** with a DC of 10 + the spell's level. On success, the spell is countered.
- The spell can be cast at a higher slot level: automatically counters spells up to the slot level used.

### Dispel Magic (3rd Level)
- Target one creature, object, or magical effect within 120 ft.
- Automatically ends spells of 3rd level and lower on the target.
- For higher-level spells: make an **ability check using your spellcasting ability** with DC = 10 + the spell's level. On success, the spell ends.
- Cast at a higher slot level: automatically ends spells up to the slot level used.

## Spell Schools

| School | Nature of Magic |
|---|---|
| **Abjuration** | Protective wards, barriers, magical negation |
| **Conjuration** | Summoning creatures, creating objects, teleportation |
| **Divination** | Revealing information, perceiving distant things |
| **Enchantment** | Affecting minds, controlling behavior |
| **Evocation** | Channeling raw energy (fire, lightning, force) |
| **Illusion** | Deceiving the senses, creating false perceptions |
| **Necromancy** | Life force manipulation, undeath, death magic |
| **Transmutation** | Altering the properties of matter and energy |
