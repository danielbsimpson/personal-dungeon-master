# D&D 5e SRD — Conditions

*Source: System Reference Document 5.1 (CC-BY-4.0, Wizards of the Coast)*

Conditions alter a creature's capabilities in defined ways and arise from spells, class features, monster attacks, and other effects. A condition lasts either until it is removed by a stated mechanism or for the duration specified by the effect that imposed it.

---

## Blinded

- A blinded creature **cannot see** and automatically **fails** any ability check requiring sight.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**.
- The creature's attack rolls have **disadvantage**.

---

## Charmed

- A charmed creature **cannot attack** the charmer or target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects.
- The charmer has **advantage on any ability check** to interact socially with the creature.

---

## Deafened

- A deafened creature **cannot hear** and automatically **fails** any ability check requiring hearing.

---

## Exhaustion

Exhaustion is measured in six levels. Each level imposes a cumulative effect.

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Disadvantage on ability checks |
| 2 | Speed halved |
| 3 | Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws |
| 4 | Hit point maximum halved |
| 5 | Speed reduced to 0 |
| 6 | Death |

- Finishing a **long rest** reduces exhaustion by 1 level, provided the creature has food and water.
- Each additional level of exhaustion gained stacks with prior levels.

---

## Frightened

- A frightened creature has **disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls** while the source of its fear is within line of sight.
- The creature **cannot willingly move closer** to the source of its fear.

---

## Grappled

- A grappled creature's **speed becomes 0** and it cannot benefit from any bonus to speed.
- The condition ends if the grappler is incapacitated.
- The condition ends if an effect removes the grappled creature from the grappler's reach (e.g., forced movement).

---

## Incapacitated

- An incapacitated creature **cannot take actions or reactions**.

---

## Invisible

- An invisible creature is **impossible to see** without magic or a special sense.
- For attack purposes, the creature's location must be guessed. **Attack rolls against it have disadvantage**; its attack rolls have **advantage**.
- An invisible creature is still detectable by noise, tracks, or smell.

---

## Paralyzed

- A paralyzed creature is **incapacitated** (cannot take actions or reactions).
- The creature **cannot move or speak**.
- The creature automatically **fails STR and DEX saving throws**.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.

---

## Petrified

- A petrified creature is transformed into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone).
- The creature is **incapacitated**, cannot move or speak, and is **unaware of its surroundings**.
- Attack rolls against it have **advantage**.
- It automatically **fails STR and DEX saving throws**.
- It has **resistance to all damage**.
- It is **immune to poison and disease**, but existing poison or disease effects are suspended.

---

## Poisoned

- A poisoned creature has **disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks**.

---

## Prone

- A prone creature's only movement option is to **crawl** (costs 1 extra ft of movement per ft travelled).
- The creature can stand up by spending movement equal to **half its speed**.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage if the attacker is within 5 ft**; otherwise they have **disadvantage**.
- The creature's own **attack rolls have disadvantage**.

---

## Restrained

- A restrained creature's **speed becomes 0** and it cannot benefit from any bonus to speed.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**; the creature's attack rolls have **disadvantage**.
- The creature has **disadvantage on DEX saving throws**.

---

## Stunned

- A stunned creature is **incapacitated** (cannot take actions or reactions).
- The creature **cannot move**, and it can only speak **falteringly**.
- The creature automatically **fails STR and DEX saving throws**.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**.

---

## Unconscious

- An unconscious creature is **incapacitated** and **cannot move or speak**.
- The creature is **unaware of its surroundings**, drops whatever it is holding, and falls **prone**.
- The creature automatically **fails STR and DEX saving throws**.
- Attack rolls against the creature have **advantage**.
- Any attack that hits is a **critical hit** if the attacker is within 5 feet of the creature.
