# D&D 5e SRD — Combat Rules

*Source: System Reference Document 5.1 (CC-BY-4.0, Wizards of the Coast)*

---

## Combat Sequence

1. **Determine surprise.** If any combatants are caught off guard, they are surprised (see below).
2. **Establish positions.** Where are everyone's creatures?
3. **Roll initiative.** Every combatant rolls DEX check (d20 + DEX modifier). Combatants act in descending order. Ties broken by higher DEX modifier; remaining ties roll off.
4. **Take turns.** Each combatant takes their turn in initiative order.
5. **Begin next round.** After all combatants have taken a turn, a new round begins. Rounds continue until combat ends.

---

## Surprise

A creature is **surprised** if it fails to notice its enemies before combat starts. The DM calls for Dexterity (Stealth) checks vs. the passive Perception of potential targets.

- A surprised creature **cannot move or take actions on its first turn** in combat.
- A surprised creature **cannot take reactions** until the end of its first turn.
- Awareness is individual — some members of a group may be surprised while others are not.

---

## Your Turn

On each turn a creature may:

| Action Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Action** | One action (Attack, Cast a Spell, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Ready, Search, Use an Object) |
| **Bonus Action** | One bonus action, if a feature grants one this turn |
| **Movement** | Up to full speed (can be split before and after actions) |
| **Free Action / Interaction** | One free object interaction (draw a weapon, open a door, etc.) |
| **Reaction** | One reaction, usable on your turn or another creature's turn when triggered |

Movement can be split around actions. You can move 10 ft, take your Action, then move the remaining distance.

---

## Actions in Combat

**Attack** — Make one melee or ranged weapon attack (or more with Extra Attack). See Attack Rolls below.

**Cast a Spell** — Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action.

**Dash** — Gain extra movement equal to your speed this turn (effectively double speed, before modifiers).

**Disengage** — Your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.

**Dodge** — Until your next turn: attackers targeting you have disadvantage on attack rolls; you have advantage on DEX saving throws. Benefit ends if incapacitated or speed drops to 0.

**Help** — Grant advantage on one ability check to an ally, or grant advantage on the next attack roll against one enemy within 5 ft.

**Hide** — Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to become hidden. You must have something to hide behind. A hidden creature's location must be guessed; attacks against unknown locations have disadvantage and miss if the wrong square is targeted.

**Ready** — Declare a trigger and an action to take when that trigger occurs. Uses your reaction when triggered. A readied spell requires concentration until released; if not triggered before your next turn, the spell slot is lost.

**Search** — Devote your attention to finding something. Make a Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check.

**Use an Object** — Interact with an object that requires more effort than a free interaction (using a consumable magic item, administering a potion to an unconscious creature, etc.).

## Attack Rolls

**To hit:** Roll 1d20 + attack modifier vs. target's AC.

- **Melee attack modifier:** STR modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
- **Ranged attack modifier:** DEX modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient)
- **Finesse weapons:** Use the higher of STR or DEX
- **Spell attack:** Spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus

A **natural 20** (d20 shows 20) is a **critical hit**.
A **natural 1** (d20 shows 1) is an **automatic miss**.

---

## Damage Rolls

On a hit, roll the damage die or dice listed for the weapon or spell and add the relevant modifier.

- **Melee weapon:** STR modifier added to damage (or DEX for finesse weapons)
- **Ranged weapon:** DEX modifier added to damage
- **Two-handed melee:** Use the weapon's versatile die
- **Off-hand (two-weapon fighting):** No ability modifier added to damage unless the character has the Two-Weapon Fighting style

Damage is reduced by resistance (halved), negated by immunity, or increased by vulnerability (doubled).

---

## Critical Hits

When you score a critical hit:
- Roll **all of the attack's damage dice twice** and add them together.
- Add modifiers **once** (not twice).

*Example: A shortsword normally deals 1d6 + 3. On a critical hit it deals 2d6 + 3.*

---

## Two-Weapon Fighting

When you take the Attack action with a light melee weapon in one hand, you can use a **bonus action** to attack with a different light melee weapon in the other hand.

- The off-hand weapon must also be light.
- You do **not** add your ability modifier to the off-hand damage roll unless you have the Two-Weapon Fighting style.

---

## Grapple

**To grapple:** Use the Attack action to make one special melee attack in place of a normal attack.
- The target must be no more than one size larger than you and within reach.
- Make a Strength (Athletics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).
- On success, the target gains the **Grappled** condition (speed becomes 0).

**To escape:** The grappled creature can use its action to make an Athletics or Acrobatics check contested by your Athletics check.

**Moving a grappled creature:** You can drag or carry the grappled creature with you, but your speed is halved unless the creature is two or more sizes smaller.

---

## Shove

**To shove:** Use the Attack action to make one special melee attack in place of a normal attack.
- The target must be no more than one size larger than you and within reach.
- Make a Strength (Athletics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).
- On success, choose: push the target 5 feet away from you, or knock the target **prone**.

---

## Ranged Attacks

- Ranged attacks have a **normal range** and a **long range** (listed as e.g. *range 80/320 ft*).
- Attacking at long range imposes **disadvantage**.
- Attacking while an enemy is within 5 ft of you imposes **disadvantage** (the enemy is threatening you, not necessarily the target).

---

## Cover

| Cover Type | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Half cover** (low wall, another creature) | +2 bonus to AC and DEX saves |
| **Three-quarters cover** (portcullis, thick tree) | +5 bonus to AC and DEX saves |
| **Full cover** | Cannot be directly targeted by attacks or spells |

---

## Opportunity Attacks

When a hostile creature **you can see** moves out of your reach, you can use your **reaction** to make one melee attack against it.

- You cannot make opportunity attacks against creatures using the Disengage action or when voluntarily teleporting.
- Forced movement (being pushed, pulled) does not trigger opportunity attacks.

---

## Death Saving Throws

When a character drops to 0 hit points:
- They fall **unconscious** and are **dying**.
- At the **start of each of their turns**, they roll a d20 (no modifiers).
- **10 or higher:** one success. **9 or lower:** one failure.
- **3 successes:** the character is **stabilized** (0 HP but no longer dying).
- **3 failures:** the character **dies**.
- A **natural 20** on a death save: the character regains 1 HP and regains consciousness.
- A **natural 1** on a death save: counts as **two failures**.
- Taking damage at 0 HP adds one failure. A critical hit at 0 HP adds two failures.
- Successes and failures reset when the character regains any HP.

---

## Stabilization

A **stable** character has 0 HP but is not making death saving throws. To stabilize a dying creature:
- Administer first aid: DC 10 Medicine check as an action.
- Cast a healing spell for any amount.
- A stable character regains 1 HP after 1d4 hours.

---

## Mounted Combat

A **willing creature** of at least one size larger than the rider can serve as a mount.

- Mounting/dismounting costs movement equal to half your speed.
- If the mount is forced to move, the rider can use a reaction to stay on or be dismounted (falling prone in a space within 5 ft).
- Falling from a mount (knocked off or mount falls prone) lands the rider prone unless they succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw.

**Controlled mounts:** Act on the same initiative as the rider. Move and use the Dash, Disengage, or Dodge action.

**Independent mounts:** Retain their own initiative. Can take any action they choose.

---

## Underwater Combat

- Creatures without a **swimming speed** have disadvantage on melee weapon attack rolls unless the weapon is a dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear, or trident.
- Ranged weapon attacks automatically miss targets beyond the weapon's normal range.
- Fire-based damage is resisted by creatures fully submerged in water.
