# Anansi the Spider

A D&D 5e one-shot designed for a single player of level 5–7, set in a West African-inspired spirit market at the edge of the mortal world. A trickster god offers to grant anything the player desires — if they are clever enough to earn it.

> For full scene descriptions, encounter details, and DM notes, see [Anansi the Spider.txt](Anansi%20the%20Spider.txt).

> For all named characters and NPC guidance, see [characters.md](characters.md).

> For all creature stat blocks and encounter notes, see [creatures.md](creatures.md).

---

## Overview

The player character arrives at **Kumasi's Edge** — a crossroads village where the mortal world brushes up against the spirit realm — seeking a boon only the spider god **Anansi** can provide: a lost soul, a stolen truth, a path that cannot be found any other way. Anansi knows they are coming. He has been watching. When he appears, he names the terms plainly: three tasks of wit, cunning, and heart — freely undertaken, forfeitable at any time. Complete them, and whatever you seek is yours. But Anansi is never simply testing what he says he is testing.

**Tone:** Whimsical menace, riddles and earned weight, moral clarity at the end
**Setting:** Kumasi's Edge and Anansi's domain within the spirit market
**Themes:** Cleverness over strength, the nature of stories, the difference between what we want and what we need

## One-Shot Design Notes

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Recommended Player Level** | 5–7 (single player) |
| **Estimated Play Time** | 3–4 hours |
| **Combat Role** | Optional — the entire campaign can be completed without a single attack roll. Task 2 is the only act with combat-capable encounters, and each has a non-combat resolution. |
| **Key DM Principle** | Anansi is never the enemy. He is the test. Voice him as genuinely delighted by a clever player and quietly disappointed by a foolish one. His approval is never stated aloud — it is only visible in the moments where his amusement deepens into something else. |
| **Tone Calibration** | Begin whimsical and light. Let the weight accumulate naturally through Task 2 and arrive fully in Task 3. The ending should feel earned, not imposed. |
| **If the Player Attacks Anansi** | He disappears. The tasks reset. The boon recedes one step further. Fighting him is always the wrong answer — but the wrong answer is still a playable outcome, and the DM should treat it with the same narrative seriousness as any other choice. |

## Kumasi's Edge

Kumasi's Edge is a crossroads village in a savanna-meets-forest landscape, built where three trade roads converge beneath the canopy of an enormous silk-cotton tree. The tree is old enough that its roots have cracked the market road and its branches cover the entire square. Traders say that if you sleep beneath it and make an honest wish, Anansi will hear it. Most people treat this as superstition. It is not.

By day, Kumasi's Edge is ordinary and lively. By night, those with business for Anansi say the roots glow faintly — the color of candlelight seen through silk.

### Key Locations

| Location | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **The Silk-Cotton Tree** | Center of the market square; the passage to the spirit market is found beneath its roots |
| **Elder Kofi's House** | First point of contact; context, warning, and the ritual required to open the passage |
| **The Weaver's Stall** | Yaa's stall — empty and locked; the first sign that something is already wrong |
| **The Crossroads Shrine** | Site of the three-part ritual that opens the passage beneath the tree |
| **The Story-Vault** | Anansi's archive within the spirit market; location of Task 1 |
| **The Tangle** | A labyrinth of living webs stretching between two spirit-world pillars; location of Task 2 |
| **The Heart Chamber** | Anansi's innermost sanctum; location of Task 3 and the final resolution |

### Key NPCs

| Name | Role |
|---|---|
| **Anansi** | The trickster god himself; architect of every task, every omission, and every outcome |
| **Elder Kofi** | Village elder; the player's only guide before entering the spirit market |
| **Yaa** | A young weaver who entered without completing the ritual; lost in the Tangle; Task 2 complication |
| **The Archivist** | A voice-spirit bound to the Story-Vault; riddle-keeper and Anansi's instrument in Task 1 |

## Campaign Structure

### Act 0 — The Bargain

The player arrives in Kumasi's Edge with their reason for seeking Anansi. **Elder Kofi** provides everything they need to enter: context about what Anansi is, a description of the three-part ritual at the Crossroads Shrine (grain, cloth, and a spoken name — the player's own), and a warning that inside the spirit market, Anansi controls everything except the player's own choices.

He also mentions **Yaa** — a weaver who went in three days ago without completing the ritual. He does not say she is his granddaughter. She is. He asks the player, simply, that if they see her, they bring her back.

Completing the shrine ritual opens the passage beneath the Silk-Cotton Tree. Descending it opens Act 1.

---

### Act 1 — The Three Tasks

Anansi appears inside the spirit market as a small, well-dressed older man in immaculate robes, with round spectacles that do not sit quite straight. He introduces the bargain. The player may forfeit at any point and leave with nothing. He is not lying about any of this.

The three tasks follow in sequence.

#### The Tasks

| Task | Name | Summary | Core Mechanic | Creatures |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Task 1** | The Task of Words | The Archivist presents three riddles drawn from stories stored in the vault. Each answer requires inference from the riddle's internal logic — not outside knowledge. | Roleplay / Arcana DC checks | None (Swarm of Spiders if the player attempts magical cheating) |
| **Task 2** | The Task of Hands | Navigate the Tangle — a living web labyrinth — and recover a stolen lantern before it burns out. Yaa is lost inside. The player must choose between the faster route and the right one. | Exploration + optional combat + one moral choice | Ettercap ×1, Giant Spider ×2, Phase Spider ×1 |
| **Task 3** | The Task of Heart | Anansi offers the player a choice: their boon exactly as they stated it, or what they actually came here needing. Choosing the literal version requires passing a DC 14 Wisdom save or losing something real. Choosing the honest version requires saying something true out loud. | Pure roleplay | None (unless the player attacks Anansi) |

---

### Act 2 — The Twist

After Task 3, Anansi tells the player the truth: the door to their boon has been open since they arrived. The three tasks were not obstacles standing between the player and what they wanted. They were the answer to the question the player came here asking — the question being not *what do you want* but *who are you*.

The resolution depends entirely on how the player handled the three tasks.

| Player Choices | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Cheated in Task 1, abandoned Yaa in Task 2, chose literally in Task 3 | Receives a technically correct version of the stated boon — functional, but hollow in exactly the way the player earned |
| Mixed choices — some integrity, some shortcuts | Receives the boon with a visible flaw that Anansi names precisely and without cruelty |
| Full integrity across all three tasks | Receives the full boon, plus Anansi's genuine respect — which he expresses by telling the player one true thing about themselves that he has been watching since they arrived |

---

### Act 3 — The Resolution

The player exits through the Silk-Cotton Tree with their boon. Yaa returns to her stall (if rescued). Elder Kofi is waiting beneath the tree exactly where they left him. He does not ask how it went — he reads the answer from the player's face and says whatever the moment requires.

Anansi is already gone. There is a single thread of spider silk tied to the player's wrist that was not there before. They will notice it later.

If the player found a genuinely better answer to one of the Archivist's riddles than Anansi's own — cleanly, without tricks — a second thread appears. Anansi does not explain this. He never will.
